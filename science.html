Encouraging Curiosity Through Hands-On Discovery

Young children are natural scientists—always observing, questioning, and exploring. At Maya Montessori, we nurture this curiosity through hands-on activities that introduce children to the natural world. From growing plants to exploring animal habitats, children build early science skills by engaging all of their senses.

What We Focus On

Observation: Children learn to slow down and really look—whether at a bug, a rock, or a flower.
Exploration: Using tools like magnifying glasses, they begin to notice textures, colors, and patterns in nature.
Classification: Sorting leaves, animals, or objects helps them make sense of the world around them.
Cause & Effect: Simple experiments show how things change—melting ice, growing seeds, or mixing colors.
Respect for Nature: Through gardening and animal care, children learn responsibility and appreciation for living things.

Science Sequence

  • Zoology: Learning about animal types, body parts, and habitats
  • Botany: Observing plants, leaves, flowers, and growing life cycles
  • Earth & Weather: Exploring natural elements like rocks, rain, clouds, and seasons
  • Physical Science: Hands-on play with light, magnets, and water
  • Gardening & Ecology: Taking care of plants and exploring composting, insects, and soil
  • Simple Experiments: Observing changes—melting, sinking, floating, and mixing
